---
title: "Firewall bypass"
description: "If your CDN's bot protection blocks the scanner, let chifu through with a secret header."
---

Hosts like **Vercel** and **Cloudflare** ship bot protection that challenges automated
traffic. That's great against attackers, but it can also block chifu's scanner,
leaving you with a thin or empty report.

The fix: chifu sends a **secret header** on every scan, and you add one firewall rule
that says "if you see this header, let it through." Attackers don't know the secret;
chifu does.

<Note>
  You only need this if your scans come back suspiciously empty or blocked. Plenty of
  sites don't need it at all. The panel only appears on **verified** domains.
</Note>

## Enable it

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the domain">
    On **Domains**, expand a verified domain and find the **Firewall bypass** panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate a secret">
    Click **Enable firewall bypass**. chifu creates a random secret and a header name
    (default `x-chifu-bypass`) and attaches them to every future scan of this domain.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy the header name and value">
    You'll need both for the next step. Use the eye icon to reveal the value.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add the rule at your CDN">
    Follow the recipe for your provider below.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Provider recipes

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Vercel">
    1. Open your project → **Firewall** → **Custom Rules** → **New Rule**.
    2. **If**: `Request Header` → name = your header (e.g. `x-chifu-bypass`) →
       `equals` → your secret value.
    3. **Then**: choose **Allow** / **Bypass** so the request skips bot protection.
    4. Save and deploy the rule.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Cloudflare">
    1. Open your domain → **Security** → **WAF** → **Custom rules** → **Create rule**.
    2. Use the expression editor:
       ```
       http.request.headers["x-chifu-bypass"][0] eq "YOUR_SECRET_VALUE"
       ```
    3. Set the action to **Skip** and select the protections to skip (managed rules /
       Bot Fight Mode).
    4. Deploy the rule.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Custom (any WAF / proxy)">
    On whatever sits in front of your site (nginx, a load balancer, another WAF), add an
    **allow** rule that matches requests where the header equals your secret, and let
    those requests bypass your bot/rate-limit protection.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Warning>
  Treat the secret like a password. Anyone with it can skip your bot protection. Don't
  commit it to a public repo or paste it in screenshots.
</Warning>

## Manage the secret

The panel shows the bypass status as **Active** or **Off**, and lets you:

- **Reveal / hide** the secret value.
- **Rotate** it: generates a new secret. Remember to update your CDN rule
  afterward.
- **Remove** it: turns bypass off and clears the header.

<Tip>
  Rotate the secret periodically, and any time you suspect it leaked. After rotating,
  update the value in your CDN rule so scans keep getting through.
</Tip>
