A **finding** is one specific issue chifu spotted: a missing header, an exposed
banner, a dependency with a known CVE. Each scan produces findings, and the
**Findings** page aggregates them across all your scans so you have one to-do
list.

## The Findings page

Open **Findings** in the sidebar to see everything chifu has flagged for your
domains. Each row shows:

- **Severity** how urgent it is (see below).
- **Finding** the title, plus its ID, any CVE, and CVSS score.
- **Target** which domain it's on.
- **Status** where it stands (Open by default).
- **Age** how long ago it was discovered.

<Note>
  Rows aren't clickable: there's no separate page per finding. Everything you
  need is on the row itself, and the full context lives on the
  [scan report](/scans) the finding came from. The table updates **live**, so a
  scan that finishes while you're looking adds its findings without a refresh.
</Note>

### Filtering

Use the **search box** to filter by title, target, or ID. Toggle the
**Critical / High / Medium / Low** chips to focus on a severity.

## Statuses

Each finding carries a status so you can track your progress:

| Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Open** | Newly found and not yet handled. |
| **Fixed** | You resolved it. |
| **Dismissed** | Not relevant to you. |
| **Accepted** | A known, accepted risk. |

## Known vulnerabilities (CVEs)

When chifu matches your detected stack to a published CVE, it shows up in the
**Known vulnerabilities** table on the scan report, with the CVE ID, severity,
affected version range, and a link to the official NVD entry. These are usually
the highest-impact items and the fastest wins: bumping a dependency version often
clears several at once.

## Working through findings

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sort by severity">
    Start with Critical and High. The dashboard already ranks them first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the plain-English explanation">
    Each finding explains what's wrong and why it matters, no jargon required.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply the fix">
    Apply the suggested fix yourself, or let your AI editor do it through the
    [chifu MCP](/ai-editor): it pulls the findings and edits your code directly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Re-scan to confirm">
    Run the scan again after you redeploy. Fixed items raise your
    [risk score](/risk-score) and show up as a green delta.
  </Step>
</Steps>
