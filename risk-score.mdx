title: "Your risk score"
description: "One number that tells you how exposed your site is, and the five things that move it."
---

After every scan, chifu boils everything it found into a single **risk score**
from **0 to 99**. The gauge is color-coded so you can read it without thinking.

## Reading the gauge

**Higher is safer.** A high score with a green band means your site is in good
shape; a low score with a red band means there's work to do.

| Band | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Excellent** | Locked down. Nothing serious to act on. |
| **Good** | Solid, with minor things worth tidying up. |
| **Fair** | Some real gaps, worth a look this week. |
| **Poor** | Multiple issues dragging you down. Fix soon. |
| **Critical** | Serious exposure. Treat as urgent. |

## The five components

The **Score breakdown** splits your score into five areas. Each gets its own 0 to
100 sub-score and a bar, and again **higher is safer**.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Vulnerabilities" icon="shield-halved">
    Known CVEs matched to the framework and dependency versions chifu detected on
    your site. This is usually the biggest mover.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="TLS" icon="certificate">
    Your HTTPS certificate, protocol version, and cipher strength.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Security headers" icon="lock">
    Response headers like `Content-Security-Policy`, `X-Frame-Options`, and HSTS,
    the ones AI scaffolding tends to skip.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Exposure" icon="eye">
    Leaked information: exposed secrets, server banners, and anything public that
    shouldn't be.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Defensive posture" icon="shield">
    How well your site pushes back, things like CORS policy and error handling.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Warning>
  **A sub-score of 0 does not mean "zero problems."** Each sub-score is 0 to 100
  where **higher means safer**. A **Vulnerabilities: 0** means chifu found serious
  issues and the sub-score bottomed out, so check the **Known vulnerabilities**
  table on the report. A *high* number is the good one.
</Warning>

## Re-scans and deltas

When you scan the same site again, chifu compares it to the previous run. If a
component changed, you'll see a **delta** next to its score: a small arrow and
number.

- **Green up** means it got better since last time.
- **Red down** means it got worse (for example, a new CVE landed in a
  dependency).

This makes it easy to spot regressions right after a deploy.

<Tip>
  Scheduled scans ([see Schedule](/schedule)) run this comparison for you on a
  cadence, so a drop in your score shows up as an alert instead of a surprise.
</Tip>
